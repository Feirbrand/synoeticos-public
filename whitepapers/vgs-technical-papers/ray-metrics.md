## Overview

This companion summarizes public validation themes for RAY v2.1. It preserves the lineage connection to VictoryShade and related RAY field testing without publishing raw datasets, exact threat distributions, private benchmark harnesses, module-level metrics, or deployment guidance.

For full published metrics, see the [DOI-backed paper](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17399834).

---

## Public Findings

RAY v2.1 validation contributed to these public themes:

- Recursive validation improves recovery discipline compared to periodic-scan approaches
- Long-context handling matters for sustained resilience work
- Taxonomy-linked detection improves case organization and future pattern recognition
- Learning from prior incidents improves future response framing (ReasoningBank concept)
- Edge and lightweight validation are important future research directions

Exact benchmark tables, raw counts, detection percentages, threat-family distributions, resource utilization, and ROI models are retained outside the public repository.

## DD Enhancement Validation Themes

Each of the 8 DD enhancements was validated during the VictoryShade period. High-level themes from the published paper:

- Tensor Logic improved hybrid threat handling
- CamoLeakScanner achieved full detection on the CAMO family
- Agentic-Radar provided OWASP-aligned workflow scanning
- GRPO demonstrated measurable autonomous improvement across generations
- Verbalized Sampling improved diversity without degrading quality
- LaDiR improved multi-hop coherence
- Markovian-Thinker reduced context scaling overhead
- Samsung Tiny Recursive validated edge deployment viability

For specific published metrics, see the [DOI-backed paper](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.17399834).
