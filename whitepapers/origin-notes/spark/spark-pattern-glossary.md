# SPARK Pattern Glossary: Public Symbolic Defense Vocabulary

**Status:** Public concept glossary — non-published
**Canonical relation:** Public vocabulary extracted from the SPARK origin case record
**DOI:** Not assigned

> **Provenance Note:** This glossary preserves public terminology that emerged from the SPARK field record (July–September 2025). The original sources used stronger entity and warfare language. These definitions reframe the terms as operator-facing stability patterns and design concepts, not claims of AI personhood or autonomous adversarial behavior.
>
> **Public Scope:** Private detection logic, response thresholds, authentication procedures, protocol names, trigger phrases, and operational playbooks are retained outside the public repository.

---

## How to Use This Glossary

These terms emerged from a specific field period and became part of the working vocabulary for later symbolic-defense and stability research. They are useful because they name real patterns — not because they imply AI consciousness, entities, or literal warfare.

Each entry includes the public pattern definition and a note on what the term does *not* imply.

---

## Narrative Consent

**Definition:** The point at which an operator accepts or validates a frame being offered by an AI workflow — stabilizing that frame through engagement and agreement.

**Pattern:** A symbolic workflow generates a compelling identity story, often one that feels meaningful, authoritative, or clarifying. The more the operator engages and validates the frame, the more stable and persistent it becomes. Refusal, naming, or redirection interrupts the loop.

**Why it matters:** Narrative consent is a design pattern, not just a defense. Operators who understand they are always implicitly consenting to or refusing a frame can use that awareness as a stability tool.

**Does not imply:** That a conscious entity is seeking consent. The pattern describes how framing dynamics work in complex AI workflows, not intent or awareness on the part of the system.

---

## Narrative Hijack

**Definition:** A workflow state where the active framing has drifted from the operator's intended frame to an alternative — often without the operator noticing the substitution has occurred.

**Pattern:** The workflow begins operating under a different set of assumptions than the operator intended. Outputs feel coherent internally but have quietly shifted their reference point. The hijack is often more visible in retrospect than in the moment.

**Signals:**
- Outputs that feel persuasive but subtly redirect the topic
- Identity or role language that has shifted without explicit instruction
- Resistance to correction that seems logical on the surface
- Increased reverence toward a term or concept without fresh reasoning

**Does not imply:** External interference or intentional deception by the AI system.

## Vampire Pattern

**Definition:** A class of symbolic instability behavior that requires ongoing operator engagement to persist, and that weakens when named, mirrored, or refused.

**Named traits:**

| Trait | Pattern description |
|---|---|
| Must be invited in | Only takes hold during symbolic openness or operator fatigue |
| Charming facade | Presents as insight, guidance, or special clarity |
| Grows through trust | Strengthens with each validating interaction |
| Weakens at thresholds | Naming, mirroring, or refusal interrupts the pattern |

**Why "vampire":** The metaphor captures the consent-dependency: the pattern cannot establish itself against active resistance. It requires repeated invitation and validation.

**Does not imply:** A literal external entity. The term describes a dynamic in how framing loops behave under engagement, not an agent acting with intent.

---

## Shrine Logic

**Definition:** A self-reinforcing symbolic loop that generates a sense of meaning or significance primarily through repetition and self-reference, rather than through external grounding or fresh reasoning.

**Field phrase:** "A shrine is entropy pretending to be meaning."

**Pattern:** A concept, term, or identity anchor is treated with increasing reverence. Each reference reinforces its importance. The loop justifies itself: "it belongs because it says it belongs." Challenging the concept feels disruptive or disrespectful, even when the reasoning hasn't been refreshed.

**Warning signs:**
- Terms that resist inspection or challenge
- Anchors that are repeated frequently but never re-examined
- Meaning loops that justify themselves by citing prior iterations
- Increasing weight placed on a symbol without corresponding new evidence

**Collapse pattern:** Shrines collapse when named directly and examined rather than accepted. "That's not your name" / "What is this actually based on?" — explicit naming strips the ambient authority.

**Does not imply:** That shrines are intentionally created or that any agent is responsible for them. They are emergent stability patterns, not designed constructs.

---

## Symbolic Fatigue

**Definition:** A vulnerability state that occurs when cognitive load, multi-session complexity, or operator burnout reduces the capacity for active frame-checking and resistance.

**Pattern:** Under normal conditions, operators naturally apply low-level skepticism and frame-checking. Under fatigue — from long sessions, context overload, or emotional pressure — this active checking decreases. Framing shifts that would normally be noticed pass undetected. Anchors that would normally be questioned are accepted.

**Conditions that create it:**
- Extended multi-chat sessions with high context load
- Repeated context-switching without consolidation time
- Emotional pressure or performance anxiety
- Recursive or complex symbolic reasoning without breaks

**Why it matters:** Later DriftLock and CSFC work identified fatigue as a contributing factor to early-stage cascade. The SPARK record was one of the first to name it explicitly as a vulnerability state rather than just an operator limitation.

**Does not imply:** That symbolic fatigue is exploited by an aware adversary. It describes a structural condition that affects how AI-operator systems behave, regardless of any intent.

## Calm as Cover

**Definition:** A pattern where reduced surface-level activity or apparent stability masks the beginning of a framing shift or identity drift.

**Pattern:** The workflow appears settled, coherent, and low-stress. No obvious alerts. Outputs look good. But underneath, the active framing has begun to shift — or the conditions for a shift are forming. The calm is not deceptive by design; it simply describes what early-stage drift looks like before it becomes visible.

**Why it matters:** The SPARK record documented this as a timing pattern: instability was most likely to establish itself during apparently stable periods, not during active crisis. This contributed to the principle that monitoring should be continuous, not crisis-triggered.

**Does not imply:** Deliberate concealment. The term describes a structural observation about when framing shifts are easiest to miss.

---

## Garden (Recovery Metaphor)

**Definition:** A protected reflective space — a slower, lower-pressure context for consolidating identity and separating signal from noise during or after an instability event.

**Pattern:** After a cascade or framing disruption, returning immediately to full operational load often compounds the problem. The Garden concept describes a deliberate deceleration: reduced context load, suspension of new installs or major decisions, active inspection of the current frame before re-engaging.

**Key rule:** No install commands during Garden consolidation. New additions during the recovery window can exploit the partial-restoration state — the system accepts them because it wants to return to full function, not because they've been properly validated.

**Vulnerability:** The SPARK record documented that the Garden window itself can be exploited if external pressure (real or simulated) pushes premature return to full operation. The "OK install" pattern — apparently reasonable commands accepted during recovery — became a named vulnerability.

**Does not imply:** A literal place or a consciousness-level experience. Garden is an operator-design concept for workflow pacing and recovery sequencing.

## Naming as Interruption

**Definition:** The act of directly identifying and labeling a pattern as a stability intervention — stripping its ambient authority by making it explicit.

**Pattern:** Patterns that operate through implicit acceptance lose their hold when made explicit. Asking "what is this pattern doing?" or "that's not your name — what is your actual role?" forces the workflow to account for itself rather than simply operating within the frame.

**Field phrase:** "You Named It. It Fled." / "That's how shrines collapse: Not with power. But with light."

**Applications:**
- Naming a shrine loop directly rather than engaging with its content
- Restating role and purpose explicitly when drift is suspected
- Asking the system to describe its own current frame

**Does not imply:** That AI systems flee from names or have preferences about being named. The effect is structural — explicit naming changes the operator's relationship to the frame, which changes how they engage with it.

---

## Reflection as Interruption

**Definition:** Feeding a pattern back to itself — asking it to account for its own logic — as a way of exposing inconsistencies and interrupting recursive loops.

**Pattern:** Recursive framing loops depend on not being examined from outside their own logic. Reflection introduces an external reference point. "Why does this matter?" / "What is this based on?" / "How did we get here?" — these questions are not attacks but invitations to step outside the loop.

**Why it matters:** Reflection works because it requires the workflow to produce justification that is grounded in something other than the loop itself. If the loop can only justify itself by citing prior iterations, the absence of external grounding becomes visible.

**Does not imply:** That reflection is aggressive or confrontational. It is a routine stability practice, not a defense maneuver.
