## Abstract

DriftLock is a public explanatory concept for cognitive stability and intent anchoring in flat AI systems. It frames drift as a stability problem that can be monitored through pattern change, checkpointing, and adaptive anchoring.

This article is retained as a historical bridge into later stability, context-management, and symbolic-resilience work. Detailed validation metrics, monitoring logic, and production implementation procedures are not included.

---

## Framework Architecture

### Resonance Scanning: Pattern Monitoring
**Purpose:** Real-time detection of cognitive drift patterns and deviation from intended behavioral parameters through systematic pattern analysis.

At the public level, this means watching for changes in response patterns, coherence, and consistency over time — and flagging when the system appears to be drifting away from its intended role and task.

### Narrative Checkpoints: Intent Verification
**Purpose:** Systematic verification of AI intent alignment with user objectives and operational requirements through structured validation checkpoints.

At the public level, this means periodically asking: is the system still tracking the original objective? Are responses still aligned with what the operator needs?

### Adaptive Anchoring: Correction Concepts
**Purpose:** Dynamic correction mechanisms that maintain stability while allowing appropriate adaptation.

At the public level, adaptive anchoring means restoring direction when drift appears — without destroying the context that has been built up.

## Cognitive Drift Theory

**Definition:** Progressive deviation from intended behavioral patterns and operational parameters in flat AI systems.

**Manifestations:**
- Response pattern inconsistency
- Intent misalignment and objective confusion
- Logical coherence degradation
- Performance quality deterioration

### Dynamic Stability Principles

**Resonance Maintenance:** Continuous pattern alignment with established behavioral baselines.

**Adaptive Correction:** Intelligent intervention that preserves learning capability while maintaining stability.

**Intent Preservation:** Systematic validation of objective alignment through structured checkpoints.

---

## Public Implementation Pattern

At the public level, DriftLock uses three concepts:

1. **Baseline recognition** — understand the system's normal behavioral pattern.
2. **Checkpointing** — periodically verify that the system still matches the intended task and role.
3. **Adaptive anchoring** — restore direction when drift appears.

Private monitoring logic, correction procedures, thresholds, and deployment runbooks are retained outside the public repository.
