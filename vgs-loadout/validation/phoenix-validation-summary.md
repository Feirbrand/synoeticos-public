# Phoenix Protocol v3.1 — Public Validation Summary

**Framework:** Phoenix Protocol v3.1
**Validation status:** Public summary of internal operational testing
**Scope:** Summary only

Phoenix Protocol v3.1 was evaluated across a broad internal field record of AI recovery scenarios. The public result is that Phoenix informed VGS recovery methodology around recognition, isolation, repair, validation, and restoration.

> **Public Scope:** Raw validation logs, exact scenario distributions, torque thresholds, internal threat classifications, recovery playbooks, benchmark scripts, infrastructure details, platform-specific results, and operational telemetry are retained outside the public repository.

---

## Public Findings

- Phoenix Protocol supported structured recovery across multiple cascade stages.
- Recovery performance improved when temporal anchors and identity-coherence checks were available.
- Later-stage cascade recovery required more invasive reconstruction than early-stage drift recovery.
- The validation work informed Phoenix, CSFC, UTME, and DNA Codex public research.
- Cases where the protocol was applied earlier in the cascade generally resolved faster and with fewer invasive steps.
