# PME v1.0 Master Bibliography

## Primary Research Foundation

### Neuroscience & Neuroplasticity

**Long-Term Potentiation (LTP)**

1. Bliss, T. V., & Lømo, T. (1973). Long-lasting potentiation of synaptic transmission in the dentate area of the anaesthetized rabbit following stimulation of the perforant path. *The Journal of Physiology*, 232(2), 331-356.
   - **Relevance:** Foundation for PME's pathway strengthening mechanism (LTP)
   - **Application:** Myelination strength (γ) calculation

2. Malenka, R. C., & Bear, M. F. (2004). LTP and LTD: An embarrassment of riches. *Neuron*, 44(1), 5-21.
   - **Relevance:** Comprehensive review of long-term synaptic plasticity
   - **Application:** Dual-mode plasticity (LTP/LTD) in PME

3. Lisman, J., Schulman, H., & Cline, H. (2002). The molecular basis of CaMKII function in synaptic and behavioural memory. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 3(3), 175-190.
   - **Relevance:** Molecular mechanisms of synaptic strengthening
   - **Application:** Biochemical basis for pathway reinforcement

---

**Motor Learning & Myelination**

4. McKenzie, I. A., Ohayon, D., Li, H., de Faria, J. P., Emery, B., Tohyama, K., & Richardson, W. D. (2014). Motor skill learning requires active central myelination. *Science*, 346(6207), 318-322.
   - **Relevance:** Myelination supports skill acquisition and motor learning
   - **Application:** PME's 24-hour myelination timeline
   - **Key Finding:** Active myelination during learning improves performance

5. Fields, R. D. (2015). A new mechanism of nervous system plasticity: Activity-dependent myelination. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 16(12), 756-767.
   - **Relevance:** Activity-dependent myelination in learning
   - **Application:** PME's usage-driven pathway optimization

6. Xiao, L., Ohayon, D., McKenzie, I. A., Sinclair-Wilson, A., Wright, J. L., Foskuhl, J., ... & Richardson, W. D. (2016). Rapid production of new oligodendrocytes is required in the earliest stages of motor-skill learning. *Nature Neuroscience*, 19(12), 1482-1491.
   - **Relevance:** Rapid myelination during skill acquisition
   - **Application:** PME's rapid pre-activation mechanism

---

**Extinction Learning & Behavioral Plasticity**

7. Bouton, M. E. (2004). Context and behavioral processes in extinction. *Learning & Memory*, 11(5), 485-494.
   - **Relevance:** Building new competing associations to overcome old patterns
   - **Application:** PME's pathway weakening (LTD) mechanism
   - **Key Principle:** Extinction through new learning, not erasure

8. Quirk, G. J., & Mueller, D. (2008). Neural mechanisms of extinction learning and retrieval. *Neuropsychologia*, 46(12), 3009-3020.
   - **Relevance:** Neural circuits underlying extinction learning
   - **Application:** PME's dual-mode plasticity (LTP/LTD integration)

9. Rescorla, R. A. (2004). Spontaneous recovery. *Learning & Memory*, 11(5), 501-509.
   - **Relevance:** Recovery of extinguished responses
   - **Application:** PME's pathway maintenance and pruning strategy

**Expertise Development**

    - **Application:** PME's pathway optimization through repeated reinforcement
    - **Key Insight:** Expert performance requires sustained pathway myelination

    - **Application:** PME's long-term pathway strengthening

12. Gobet, F., & Campitelli, G. (2007). The role of domain-specific practice, handwriting speed, and letter-writing ability in Scrabble performance: A multiple regression analysis. *Acta Psychologica*, 124(2), 200-209.

    - **Application:** PME's pathway identification by domain

    - **Application:** PME's pathway preservation during optimization

19. Kirkpatrick, J., Pascanu, R., Rabinowitz, N., Veness, J., Desjardins, G., Rusu, A. A., ... & Hadsell, R. (2017). Overcoming catastrophic forgetting in neural networks. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, 114(13), 3521-3526.
